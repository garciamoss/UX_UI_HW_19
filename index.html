UX | UI

Some hard-earned UX skills here…

…and the case studies where I cut my UI teeth

U.S. Department of Labor

For this project, each group chose an agency from a list of federal government websites to redesign; however, each group member developed their case study from collaborative research. The timeline was five weeks. Currently, the site is an impersonal, bloated labyrinth of broken links and disorientating navigation; my solution was to pare down the site navigation, using the mobile-first approach and re-establishing the agency’s focus on the American wage-earner.

AirGeorgia AAMP

Our group wanted to spread awareness of air pollutants and their effects on the population, so we created a campaign that included a subscription-based microsite, a mobile prototype, and a wearable (smartwatch) prototype. My roles were UX researcher, UI designer (mobile), and FE developer (microsite). The period for the project was three weeks.

Himalayan Children's Charities

My group chose HCC as its nonprofit site redesign case study; it lacked solid IA, visual appeal, and navigational focus. Our solution was to redo the navbar, pare down the text-heavy content into infographics, and showcase the beautiful photographs of HCC students and their Himalayan surroundings. My roles: UX researcher, UI designer, and Google Slides designer.

Graphic Design

Continental Cuisine

The chef/owner of a Washington, D.C. personal chef business wanted to emphasize the individual service he provides to his clients; his stylized portrait certainly set him apart from others in the same business. Collateral work included brochures, a stationery suite, and eventually a website.

LETR logos

The Special Olympics of Georgia (SOGA) selected these designs for pins and hats celebrating the Law Enforcement Torch Run (LETR). Merchandise sold by SOGA and local law enforcement agencies helps raise funds for the resilient and inspirational athletes participating in the annual Summer Games.

Pampered Pooches Day

This event is co-sponsored by 2nd Chance 4 Pets, a non-profit organization which rescues pets whose owners could no longer care for them. I looked to my Lab, Addis, for inspiration; he was my mother's dog whom we adopted after she passed away.

Atlanta Bicycle Campaign

This design was the winning entry of a t-shirt design contest sponsored by the Atlanta Bicycle Campaign for its annual Bike There Ride & Festival at Emory University. The organization’s logo, event name, and the phrase “Give Cyclists a Way to Go” were required design elements.

Ole Miss/Ducks Unlimited

I designed this series as part of a proficiency test for a job at an athletic gear warehouse. The test taught me how to quickly plan a layout and revealed several time-saving methods with the software used for the project.

UGA Spillin' the Tea

Spillin’ the Tea—SEC Style t-shirt line was a continuation of the proficiency test. Here, I conceived a uniquely Southern and contemporary series involving the popular Southern beverage and a twist on an urban idiom (plus a playful diss on the Vols—apologies to Tennessee fans [not]).

REI poster

I create some branding exercises to put my stamp on to keep things fresh. The existing REI logo is a starting point to enhance its presence in the outdoor gear market. Emphasis is on the REI customer— an outdoor enthusiast participating in several activities such as those shown here

La Boucherie

Another proficiency test, this time for a position at a sign company. Required elements included the photograph and logo and were copied for a 24”x36” poster. I received another interview to explain the decisions and process involved in this poster’s production.

Jenny Holzer Truism

Most of Jenny Holzer’s work is text art, with Truisms as her best-known series. I selected one Truism and found imagery that would imbue a different meaning to the original statement. The photograph was shot in color; I changed it to grayscale except for the eyes to reveal the soul.

Twinings Tea

A friend recommended Twinings Tea as the focus for my food packaging/branding assignment; it's a dominant brand in her native England but not well-known here in the States, though widely available in grocery chains such as Kroger and Publix. My approach is to give Twinings, a 300-year-old brand, a quintessentially English High-Tea profile.

House of Earth Trilogy

I read Pearl S. Buck’s The Good Earth in high school, but I wasn’t aware it was part of a trilogy. The cover assignment allowed me to correct this oversight. I wanted to simulate the bamboo scroll paintings I saw at a museum exhibition. However, because the trilogy chronicles China's transformation into a significant 20th century world power, I may revisit the assignment.

Azzurra Brand Collateral

The brand collateral exercise focused on a fictional airline, Azzurra (Italian for blue). Azzurra’s brand identity started with a logo, then expanded to airline tickets, baggage labels, brochures, and a jet prototype. This is another project worthy of a revisit, this time to apply UX/UI practices.

Digital Media

Continental Cuisine website

The Continental Cuisine website is my first web design assignment—ever! This meant that the project was also my introduction to HTML, CSS, Javascript snippets, and the joy (!) of coding from scratch. Even then, I was mindful of clean navigation and the user experience/interface, but more akin to the K.I.S.S. philosophy. https://garciamoss.github.io/continental_cuisine/.

King's Battery Mountaineers

Yes, this is a website for a Civil War reenactment group that frequently portrayed a Confederate artillery unit. My collaboration with the group members resulted in the site’s creation to recruit other enthusiasts as living historians faithfully recreating Civil War events; their activities were akin to reenactors at historical places such as Williamsburg or Plymouth Plantation. https://garciamoss.github.io/kings_battery/

The Edifice Group

As a production artist for Edifice Group, I designed two sales email campaigns each month and one service campaign for the dozen (or more) automotive dealerships assigned to me. Before campaign layouts, the artists researched each dealership’s inventory; the campaigns must also meet compliance set by the OEMs before deployment.

Mustang eMail

I conceived this email campaign primarily from an acquaintance's desire to purchase a Mustang. After asking him a few questions about what he looks for in both the car and his buying experience, I designed and coded the email template, then had him test it on his mobile. I don’t yet know whether he bought the Mustang but at least he’s informed of several options.

Range Rover Emails

I saw a job opening for an email designer at a local dealership. After the initial interview, I designed these Land Rover emails, one of which tied in with the Bond movie premiering that weekend. From the follow-up interview, I learned about personas and user stories and how these guide the design and deployment of the dealership’s email campaigns.

Napa Website Prototype

Though these landing page layouts were part of a proficiency test, I learned how quickly I could create demos and prototypes in a pinch (thanks to Bootstrap and a bit of CSS). One landing page expanded to a complete website prototype with eleven pages in three days, though I only sent the original single page for consideration.

Custom Brokerage PowerPoint

Before Zoom meetings became mainstream, an acquaintance collaborated with me to produce several Powerpoint presentations for her classes on Custom Brokerage. The profession is part of a heavily regulated industry requiring compliance with several federal agencies; the content was consequently text-heavy—my mission to make the presentations more visually appealing.

Mammoth Sales Presentation

In this PowerPoint presentation, I designed the layout according to the new branding standards the company approved just a few weeks prior. The clients’ and their customers’ names were also changed for confidentiality. The original presentation consisted of 130 slides; I reduced it to fewer than 80 after I grouped repeating elements into several categories.

Metro Subway site

I took an Information Architecture course while a student at Portfolio Center. The assignment directed students to apply IA principles while designing a website layout. Those IA best practices still resonate years later as I progressed through UX/Ui Boot Camp. Note the Netscape browser in the prototypes, by the way.
