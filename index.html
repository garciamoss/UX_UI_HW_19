Hi, I'm Lisa.

As a freelancing Atlanta-based designer/illustrator, I have over ten years’ experience creating visually appealing illustrations, logos, printed ephemera, and websites for various small- and medium-sized businesses and nonprofits. After graduating from Georgia Tech’s UX | UI Bootcamp in June 2022, I hope to join your team as a newly minted User Experience | User Interface Designer and researcher.

UX | UI

Know Your Audience. Though I've been mindful of this maxim since design school, UX|UI Bootcamp has taken me to a deeper, more rigorous user research. Methods such as Competitor Analysis, User Interviews, Personas, Affinity Diagrams and Empathy Maps allow me to both grow creatively as a designer and develop better insight as a sort of social scientist.

Through development techniques and collaboration/coordination with team members and stakeholders, I sketch prototypes then digitally iterate those via InVision, Figma, and XD.Bottom line: Form and function must also meet the needs of both user and stakeholder.

HTML 5, CSS 3, jQuery, and Bootstrap/Responsive Design; this portfolio site is just the latest undertaking. Please peruse the case studies below for further details how these projects developed from briefs to high fidelity designs that are both visually appealing and technically practical.

Himalayan Children's Charities

Minimal use of photos, text-heavy content, and inadequate navigation of the site were the major pain points. Direct collaboration with HCC's founder, stunning photographs of the HCC students and the Himalayan scenery, and infographics improved the site’s aesthetics, content, and navigation. Click below for a detailed narrative.

Department of Labor

Broken links, uninformative taxonomy, and navigation disorientation are only a few of challenges encountered by visitors entering this labyrinth that is the DOL site. Less text and more photos/infographics, and user-centric content/navigation are among the solutions to tackle this behemoth.
